The part AI can't do for you: the outreach.

The one number that decides everything

15–20 outreach touches. Every single day.

Not hours watched. Not tools collected. Touches sent. Cold conversion is ~1–5%, so 100 contacts ≈ 1–5 clients. Most people quit at 12 — the ones who get paid simply sent the other 88.

Step 1 · Build the list

The Google Maps method — 100 leads

  1. Open Google Maps. Search [your niche] near [your city] — e.g. dentist near Austin, then nearby towns.
  2. Click each listing. You're hunting for the gap: no website, an ugly/outdated one, or no online booking. That gap is your pitch.
  3. Drop them in leads-tracker.csv: name, phone, the gap, contact, social. Note the gap in plain words ("site looks 2009, not mobile").
  4. Aim for 100 rows before heavy outreach. Businesses with reviews but a weak web presence are gold — they clearly want customers and are visibly losing some.
Warm-lead shortcut: for your best 5–10, pre-build the demo first (clone one of your niche demos, swap in their name + a couple of their real Google reviews) and lead with "I already built you something — want to see it?" That converts far better than a promise.

Step 2 · Reach out

The scripts — steal these

Swap the [placeholders]. Always lead with the gap and the lost money, never the tech. Then offer to show, not tell.

Instagram / Facebook DM Hey [name] — love what you're doing at [business]. Quick thing: I went to book/look you up and noticed [the gap — e.g. there's no website / no online booking], which is probably sending a few customers to competitors.

I actually built you a quick example of what a modern site + an AI that answers missed messages could look like. Want me to send the link? No pitch, just take a look. 🙂
Cold email Subject: a website I made for [business] (free to look)
Subject alt: you're losing calls after hours, [name]

Hi [name],
I noticed [business] [the gap] — so I built a quick example of a modern site for you, plus an AI assistant that answers every missed call and books the customer in automatically.

Here's the live preview: [demo link]

If you like it, I can have your real version live in about a week. Worth a 10-minute call?
— [your name], [phone]
Cold call / walk-in opener "Hi, is the owner around? — Hey [name], I'll be quick. I help [dentists] in [city] stop losing customers from missed calls and a weak website. I actually already mocked up what yours could look like. Can I text or email you the link to look at later today?"

Goal of the call isn't to close — it's to get the demo in front of them.
Voicemail (keep it 12 seconds) "Hi [name], it's [your name] — I build websites and AI booking assistants for [niche] and I made a quick example for [business]. I'll text you the link. Take a peek, no pressure. Thanks!"

Step 3 · Handle the "no"

Objections, answered

"We're too busy / we're fine" "Totally fair — that's exactly why I built it for you instead of asking you to do anything. The whole point is it runs without you. Want me to just send the link and you decide later?"
"How much?" "Setup is [$500–$2,000] one-time, then [$150–$500]/month to host it and run the AI. But look at the demo first — if it wouldn't pay for itself in saved bookings, don't do it."
"Just send me info" "Will do — the best 'info' is the live demo, it'll make more sense than any brochure. Sending now. Mind if I check back Thursday?"
"I already have a website" "Nice — does it answer your phone at 9pm and book the customer? That's the part I add. I'll send the example so you can see the difference."

Step 4 · Follow up & close

The cadence + the cash rule

  1. Day 0: Send the demo link.
  2. Day 2: "Did you get a chance to peek at it? Happy to tweak anything."
  3. Day 5: Share a quick win — "Added your real reviews to it, take a look."
  4. Day 9: The takeaway — "Should I keep your demo live or take it down? No worries either way."
  5. On yes: Take the deposit via Stripe before you build. Cash first, always. Then deliver fast with AI, over-communicate, and ask for a testimonial + referral the day you go live.
